與武田正吾的忘年之會 —— 一段跨越國界與世代的和牛傳奇

A cross-generational encounter with Shogo Takeda — a Wagyu legend spanning borders and generations

Chapter I

不僅僅是牛肉，是時間的藝術

Beyond Beef: The Art of Time

在昆士蘭州無邊無際的藍天下，連綿的草場延伸至地平線的盡頭，正迎來一位特殊的客人。

我站在圍欄邊，看著遠處那一群毛色烏亮、體態渾圓的和牛，正悠閒地在陰影處反芻。身旁站著一位身形清瘦、目光卻異常深邃的老者。他戴著一頂洗得發白的闊邊草帽，雙手粗糙卻有力，那是長年與牲畜、土地打交道留下的勳章。

他，就是被譽為「澳洲和牛之父」的武田正吾（Shogo Takeda）老先生。

很多人覺得澳洲的土地太烈，養不出日本那種細膩的和牛。但他們忘了，和牛的基因裡有一種韌性。在澳洲，我們不是在複製日本，我們是在利用這片土地的生命力，去喚醒基因裡的極致。

九〇年代，正是這位老先生打破了傳統的藩籬，將珍貴的和牛基因帶出了日本，才有了如今澳洲和牛在國際舞台上的盛世。

Chapter I

Under the boundless blue canopy of Queensland, where rolling pastures stretch to the edge of the horizon, a very special guest has arrived.

Standing by the fence, I watch a herd of Wagyu—their coats obsidian-black and their frames sturdy—leisurely ruminating in the shade. Beside me stands a lean elder with an exceptionally piercing gaze. He wears a faded, wide-brimmed straw hat, and his hands are rough yet powerful—medals of honor earned from a lifetime spent with livestock and the land.

This is Shogo Takeda, the man revered as the "Father of Australian Wagyu."

Many believe the Australian terrain is too harsh to produce the delicate Wagyu found in Japan. But they forget that there is a resilience within Wagyu genetics. In Australia, we aren't merely replicating Japan; we are utilizing the vitality of this land to awaken the ultimate potential within those genes.

In the 1990s, it was this man who broke through traditional barriers to bring precious Wagyu genetics out of Japan, paving the way for the golden age of Australian Wagyu on the global stage.

Queensland Ranch / 昆士蘭牧場
Chapter II

血統與平衡的哲學

The Philosophy of Lineage and Balance

我向武田先生請教關於育種（Breeding）的迷思。在當今追逐高油花（BMS）的市場中，該如何保持品質的穩定？

年輕人，你要看的不只是油花。血統的組合就像調製雞尾酒。但馬牛（Tajima）帶來極致的細膩度與不飽和脂肪酸，但牠們體型小、生長慢；氣高（Kedaka）或島根（Shimane）血統則能提供骨架與產肉率。如果你只追求油花，最後你會得到一頭虛弱的牛，那樣的肉，是沒有靈魂的。

他強調，真正的專業在於「平衡」。一個頂尖的牧場主，必須像藝術家一樣，在 EBV（預估育種值）數據與實體牛隻的表現之間，找到那個完美的交匯點。

Chapter II

I asked Mr. Takeda about the common misconceptions in breeding. In a market currently obsessed with high marbling (BMS), how does one maintain consistent quality?

Young man, you must look at more than just the marbling. Combining bloodlines is like crafting a cocktail. Tajima brings extreme fineness and unsaturated fatty acids, but they are small and slow-growing. The Kedaka or Shimane lines provide the frame and the carcass yield. If you chase only marbling, you eventually end up with a weak animal. That kind of meat has no soul.

He emphasizes that true mastery lies in balance. A top-tier rancher must act as an artist, finding the perfect intersection between EBV (Estimated Breeding Value) data and the physical performance of the animal.

Art of Marbling / 油花的藝術
Chapter III

三十個月的耐力賽

A 30-Month Marathon of Endurance

當我們巡視到育肥區時，武田先生停下了腳步。這裡的和牛已經進入了最後的肥育階段。

穀飼天數是數據，但耐心是修養。從犢牛斷奶到肥育出欄，這是一場長達 30 到 42 個月的馬拉松。在澳洲，我們有廣闊的空間讓牠們在初期發育骨骼與肌肉，但在最後的階段，飼料配方的精準度——穀物的濕度、微量元素的添加、甚至是環境的安靜程度——決定了脂肪如何像雪花一樣滲透進纖維。
你餵給牠什麼，肉就會告訴你什麼。牛是不會撒謊的。
Chapter III

As we inspect the feedlot area, Mr. Takeda pauses. These Wagyu are entering their final finishing stage.

The number of grain-fed days is a statistic, but patience is a virtue. From weaning to slaughter, this is a marathon lasting 30 to 42 months. In Australia, we have the vast space to allow them to develop bone and muscle in the early stages. But in the final phase, the precision of the ration—the moisture of the grain, the addition of micro-elements, even the quietness of the environment—determines how the fat permeates the fiber like falling snow.
Whatever you feed the animal, the meat will tell the tale. A cow does not lie.
30 Months of Patience / 三十個月的耐心
Chapter IV

傳承的火種

The Spark of Legacy

黃昏時分，夕陽將澳洲的紅土染成了金紫色。武田先生在離開莊園前，送給我一句話：

專業知識可以從書本獲取，但對生命的敬畏，只能從圍欄裡學到。你要懂牛肉，就得先聽懂牛的呼吸。

我意識到，這座牧場裡流動的不僅僅是全球頂尖的基因序列，更是一種對食物極致追求的人文精神。在澳洲這片廣袤的土地上，日本的匠心與大洋洲的豪邁，正透過一頭頭和牛，共同譜寫著屬於這個時代的食味傳奇。

Chapter IV

At dusk, the sunset paints the Australian red soil in shades of gold and violet. Before leaving the estate, Mr. Takeda leaves me with one final thought:

Technical knowledge can be acquired from books, but a reverence for life can only be learned within the fences. If you want to understand beef, you must first learn to hear the cattle breathe.

I realized then that what flows through this ranch is more than just world-class genetic sequences; it is a humanistic spirit dedicated to the ultimate pursuit of food. On this vast Australian land, Japanese craftsmanship and Oceanic boldness are weaving together a culinary legend of our time through every single animal.

Origin

和牛的起源

The Origin of Wagyu

古日本
和牛的祖先隨著稻作文化傳入日本，在封閉的島國環境中獨立演化，逐漸形成獨特的肌內脂肪沉積能力。
Ancestors of Wagyu arrived with rice cultivation in ancient Japan. In the isolated island environment, they evolved independently, gradually developing their unique ability for intramuscular fat deposition.
明治時期
日本政府開始系統性育種，確立了黑毛和種、褐毛和種、日本短角種和無角和種四大品系，奠定了現代和牛的基因基礎。
The Japanese government began systematic breeding, establishing the four major breeds — Japanese Black, Japanese Brown, Japanese Shorthorn, and Japanese Polled — laying the genetic foundation for modern Wagyu.
1990s
武田正吾先生突破傳統藩籬，將和牛基因帶出日本。澳洲廣袤的土地與純淨的環境，為和牛提供了全新的生長空間，澳洲和牛的傳奇由此展開。
Shogo Takeda broke through traditional barriers to bring Wagyu genetics out of Japan. Australia's vast land and pristine environment provided a new home for Wagyu, and the legend of Australian Wagyu began.
Today
和牛莊園承襲武田先生的信念，在澳洲紅土上以匠心育牛，以耐心等候，讓每一塊和牛都成為時間與土地的藝術結晶。
Wagyu Estate carries on Mr. Takeda's conviction — raising cattle with craftsmanship on Australia's red earth, waiting with patience, so every cut of Wagyu becomes a crystallization of time and land.
